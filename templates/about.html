Source of Dataset

The dataset is released by Kaggle, who hosted a competition for the machine learning community to forecast the use of a city bikeshare system in Washington, D.C. The source data comes from Capital Bikeshare .

Accessibility of the Dataset

The dataset released by Kaggle is open-source and available to the public on Internet. Kaggle requires the users to sign in to obtain the data, but Capital Bikeshare's official site also provides free downloadable files of the trip data.

Summary of the Dataset

The dataset contains data points taken on 731 days in 2011 and 2012. The variable of interest is the count of total bike rentals each day, and most related data fields include

date - Year, Month, and Day
season - 1 = spring, 2 = summer, 3 = fall, 4 = winter
holiday - whether the day is considered a holiday
workingday - whether the day is neither a weekend nor holiday
weather - 1 = Clear / Partly cloudy, 2 = Mist, 3 = Light rain/ snow, 4 = Heavy rain /snow
temperature - temperature in Celsius
feeling temperature - feeling temperature in Celsius
humidity - relative (normalized) humidity
windspeed - normalized wind speed
registered - number of registered user rentals initiated
